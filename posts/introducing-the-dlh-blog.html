Data Lab Hell (DLH) is an extra-university research institution and R&D Incubator & Innovation Lab based in Zirl, Austria. We are not profit-oriented — we reinvest our earnings entirely into research and development — and we work consistently as a cooperation partner with companies and research institutions.

Most of what we do lives inside project reports, confidential deliverables, and internal notebooks. That felt like a missed opportunity. This blog is our attempt to change that.

What we work on

Our work sits at the interface of science and industry — with the depth of a research institution and the agility of a startup, without being either. Concretely, that means:

  • Data-based methods for industrial applications
  • Decentralised and adaptive algorithms for complex real-world systems
  • Mathematical and physical models for new problem domains
  • Experimental platforms that bridge theory and applied use

The team is deliberately interdisciplinary: mathematicians, statisticians, computer scientists and physicists working together on problems that don't fit neatly into one discipline.

Why we are writing publicly

Applied research produces a lot of knowledge that never reaches anyone outside the immediate project. Methods that worked, approaches that didn't, findings that are genuinely interesting but don't fit into a paper — all of it tends to stay internal.

We want to share more of that. Not polished whitepapers, but honest writeups about the problems we find interesting, the tools and methods we use, and what we learn along the way.

What to expect

Posts will cover three areas:

  • Research — methods, models, and findings from our projects
  • Engineering — how we build the data pipelines, APIs, and tools that support our work
  • Company — team updates, new collaborations, and broader context on what we are doing
